# Quiet-hours + fullscreen mute so background toasts stop nagging

Kategori: protection | Efor: M | Rank: 9

## Ne
A single notification gate every ShowBalloonTip call site in MainForm.cs routes through (USB-plugged line 70, scan-summary line 302, watch-escalation line 474), suppressing non-urgent tray BalloonTips during a user-set quiet window and whenever a fullscreen app is foreground (P/Invoke SHQueryUserNotificationState; suppress on BUSY / PRESENTATION_MODE / RUNNING_D3D_FULL_SCREEN). Suppressed background threat alerts are queued and replayed as one grouped toast when state returns to normal; auto-quarantine of high-detection threats (line 338) still fires regardless of the gate. New Settings QuietHoursStart/End and MuteInFullscreen, surfaced in SettingsControl beside the existing NotifyOnThreat / NotifyMinDetections toggles.

## Neden
Today every ShowBalloonTip fires unconditionally — a USB or scan-summary toast interrupting a game or presentation is exactly the noise that drives users to disable notifications entirely, losing real protection. Gating keeps protection on while killing interruptions, and grouped replay means no threat alert is silently dropped.
